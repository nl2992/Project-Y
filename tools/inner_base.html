How this dashboard is built
Every number traces back to the fields on your uploaded blotter. Nothing is fabricated — figures are sums and counts of ticket-level fields, and each visual is a grouping of the same rows.
Metric definitions
Est. bank revenue = PC + VA/GNBV — the sum of pc_usd and va_gnbv_usd on each ticket. It is the single revenue figure everything rolls up from.
Volume uses tv_usd, falling back to primary_amount where trade value is absent.
Reoffer is the price field, quoted per 100 for note/bond products (~par); swap-style products carry none.
Asset class and remaining tenor are derived — asset class from tier1/tier2_product_type, tenor from maturity_date vs report date.
Where each visual is fed from
KPI cards — sums / counts across the current period and filters, compared to the same window one year prior.
Revenue breakdown — the Group-by dimension aggregated by est. bank revenue, ranked, vs. prior year.
Composition & monthly — revenue split by derived asset class, and revenue summed by trade_date month.
Asset-class tabs — the same rows scoped to one class, then re-grouped across every field that varies within it.
Desk copilot — reads the current aggregates plus retrieved desk reference notes; it recommends chart specs, but the dashboard computes and draws them from your rows. The free-text box turns a typed request ("revenue by client", "top 5 currencies by volume") into the same kind of chart, computed the same way.
Upload history (Golden record tab) — an append-only audit log of every upload, paste, firm sync and clear, kept even across a workspace reset.
Required fields (the reliable backbone)
trade_id · trade_date · legal_entity · treats_acronym · salesperson_coverage · tier1/2/3_product_type · primary_ccy · pc_usd · va_gnbv_usd · revenue_ccy · primary_amount — trade_id alone anchors de-duplication: a row is a duplicate, and never counted, if its Trade ID already exists anywhere in the store.
