01Quick start

From an empty board to a full read in under a minute.

  1. Open the HTML file in any modern browser (double-click, or drag it onto a browser tab).
  2. Click Upload data → choose an .xlsx/.csv, or paste CSV/TSV straight into the box, then Parse & import.
  3. The board populates instantly. Use the tabs to move between the Overview and each asset-class view.
  4. Adjust the date range and filters, or open Golden record to see and export every stored trade.
Column headers map themselves. Common names — Trade ID, Trade Date, Notional, GNBV, Product Type, Sales Client… — are recognised automatically and normalised into the desk's 44-column standardiser template. Trades are unique on Trade ID alone: re-import the same file and duplicates are skipped, never double-counted.

02What the board does

Every number is derived from your imported rows.

📥

Ingest anything flat

Upload .xlsx/.xls/.csv or paste delimited text. Headers auto-map; extra columns are preserved, not dropped.

🗂️

Golden record

One de-duplicated store of every trade. Export it back out in the exact 44-column standardiser format — it round-trips straight back in.

🎛️

Filters & presets

Date range (Weekly / Monthly / YTD presets that track today), asset class, legal entity, coverage, and free-text search.

📊

Headline KPIs

Total volume, PC, VA/GNBV, average reoffer, tickets and upload batches — each with a year-on-year delta.

🧭

Overview analytics

Revenue breakdown by any dimension, composition by asset class, monthly trend, PC-vs-GNBV split, legal-entity × asset-class matrix, and Tier-1 × Tier-2 product mix.

📑

Per-desk summary tables

Each asset-class tab reproduces the desk's Excel summary sheets: a yearly summary and a client-segment year-on-year (HK GPB vs HK External).

🔍

Data quality & exceptions

Flags missing trade dates, maturities before trade date, and negative notionals — so you catch bad rows before they skew a number.

🌓

Comfortable to read

Light & dark themes, comfortable/compact density, and a clean print layout that never clips rows.

03The AI copilot

A desk analyst that reads only what's on screen and cites the numbers.

The copilot lives on the Overview tab and works on your current filtered view — it analyses the pre-computed aggregates of exactly the rows you're looking at, grounded in the desk's own reference notes (revenue methodology, product taxonomy, tenor risk, client concentration, data quality). It never invents data. There are two ways to use it:

💡

Generate insights

One click. Returns a headline read, a few summary bullets, tone-coded cards (positive / watch / risk / neutral), suggested visuals, and clickable drill-down filters.

📈

Generate visual

Describe a chart in plain English and it's built from your current rows — e.g. “revenue by client”, “top 5 currencies by volume”, “tickets by tenor”.

Offline behaviour. The copilot calls the firm's language-model endpoint. With no backend reachable (e.g. opened from file://), it fails gracefully — you'll see a short “unable to reach the copilot” note and the rest of the board keeps working normally. No feature blocks on it.

04Good to know

  • Stuck on “No trades match the current filters”? Click Reset filters & show all dates — it widens the date window to span every loaded trade and clears the other filters.
  • Nothing is fabricated. An empty board stays empty until you feed it a file, paste, or firm sync.
  • Duplicates. Uniqueness is Trade ID alone — safe to re-import overlapping files.
  • Export twice. Export CSV gives you the current filtered view; Export golden record gives every stored trade in the 44-column template.
  • Self-contained. The file bundles everything it needs; the only network calls it ever makes are to the firm's own endpoints, and each degrades gracefully.
